About Me

Hi, my name is Joseph DiCioccio. I am a undergraduate computer science student at the University of Connecticut. I am currently in my second year, estimated to graduate in 2022. I think UConn is a great school and I enjoy it alot there.

Why UConn?

While I was still in high school and deciding on different colleges, UConn was one of my target schools. It was a school that was within my reach academically, and offered many additional benefits that some other state schools just didn't have. Although I was also accepted to some out-of-state schools, I decided that I wanted to stay closer to my hometown of Wethersfield, in which I had spent all of my life in so far. I also was told that UConn had an exceptional Computer Science department, and I have not yet been disappointed.

UConn Class of 2022 Group Picture - Im in there somewhere!

Why Computer Science?

Ever since I took my first programming class in high school (AP Computer Science A), I knew that I wanted a programming-related career. Programming not only quenced my thirst for problem-solving, but also provided a creative outlet. Most people think of programmers as people who simply sit in front of a computer all day, inside, staring at a dark screen with all sorts of foreign text and binary code. But to me, programming is a constructive practice in which I can create anything I want. One of my favorite parts about building this website was the front-end design style, playing around with different fonts, colors, and formmating until I am satisfied with the result. Above all however, programming is a field that is constantly evolving, with new things being discovered often. This field suits me, a person who never wants to stop learning new things.
